OVERVIEW

A dedicated space for sharing and discovering recipes.

YumDaily is a team-based academic project that explores how recipe content often gets lost in general social media feeds, making it difficult to organize and revisit.

We designed YumDaily as a dedicated social platform with a warm, intuitive experience, making it easier to create, share, and discover recipes.

MY ROLE

UI/UX Designer (Team of 7)

SCOPE

Contributed to the full UI/UX design process, including UX research, wireframing, mockup, prototyping, and usability testing across both web and mobile platforms. Collaborated closely with team members to ensure consistency and refine the overall user experience.

TIMELINE

7 weeks | October – November 2024

THE CHALLENGE

Why create another social platform?

Food lovers like us scatter their recipes across screenshots, bookmarks, and notes apps because existing platforms don't offer a dedicated space for culinary content. Recipe posts get buried in general social feeds, and there's no easy way to organize, revisit, or share them with a like-minded community.

As a student team, we wanted to prove we could identify a real user need and follow a full design process to address it—not just create something that looks pretty. The goal was to deliver a cross-platform experience with a warm, user-centered feel.

HYPOTHESIS

If we create a warm, community-focused space for recipe sharing…

…users will move beyond passive scrolling and actively engage - sharing their own recipes, commenting on others' posts, and building connections around food. We believed that a dedicated platform with the right social features could turn isolated home cooks into a thriving culinary community.

Goals

  • 01
    Structured recipe creation with clear steps and ingredients
  • 02
    Discover new recipes based on personal taste and dietary preferences
  • 03
    Community-driven interactions through sharing, commenting, and messaging

DESIGN PROCESS

From 183 survey answers to a warm, community-first platform

STEP 01 — RESEARCH & DISCOVERY

Validating our assumptions with real data

We started broad—surveying food communities with a Google Form. 183 responses later, our initial hypothesis shifted completely. Users didn't just want a recipe saver; they wanted connection.

Key insight: 78% said they'd use a dedicated social platform for food. We synthesized findings into 3 user personas and mapped out key pain points: scattered storage, lack of community, poor discovery.

Google Form survey — 183 responses, 82.5% aged 18-25

STEP 02 — WIREFRAMES

From low-fi sketches to structured wireframes

Based on our research insights, we explored multiple layout directions for core features such as the feed, recipe creation, and user profiles. The goal was to find a balance between content discovery and structured information.

Early wireframes revealed that overly dense layouts made it difficult for users to scan and follow recipes. We simplified the structure, improved visual hierarchy, and refined navigation flows to create a more intuitive and focused experience.

STEP 03 — VISUAL DESIGN & PROTOTYPE

Building a warm, cohesive system

With the structure validated, we moved to high-fidelity screens. The goal was to create a warm, inviting feel — so we chose a palette of orange, azureish blue, and pastel yellow. We designed 30+ screens for both mobile and web, ensuring consistency through a shared component library.

The final interactive prototype was tested again, with users praising the "welcoming vibe" and intuitive navigation.

📊 Impact (even as a student project)

While YumDaily wasn't launched, usability testing gave us clear signals. All 5 test participants completed core tasks (creating a recipe, finding a saved post, commenting) with minimal guidance. 4/5 said they'd use YumDaily if it were a real app.

💡 Key learnings

The biggest lesson? User research changes everything. Our initial "recipe saver" idea shifted completely after 183 survey responses showed people wanted community. Skipping research would have meant building something nobody needed.

Another takeaway: keeping a design system consistent across 30+ screens and 7 team members was challenging at first, but totally worth it — YumDaily feels cohesive, not like a collection of individual screens.

📈 What I'd measure next

If this shipped, I'd track: weekly active users, recipe shares per user, and time spent in the app. The goal would be to see if YumDaily truly becomes a daily companion for home cooks, not just another app they open once.
