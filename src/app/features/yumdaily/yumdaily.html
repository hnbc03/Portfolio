OVERVIEW

A dedicated space for sharing and discovering recipes.

YumDaily is a team-based academic project that explores how recipe content often gets lost in general social media feeds, making it difficult to organize and revisit.

We designed YumDaily as a dedicated social platform with a warm, intuitive experience, making it easier to create, share, and discover recipes.

MY ROLE

UI/UX Designer (Team of 5)

SCOPE

Contributed to the full UI/UX design process, including UX research, wireframing, mockup, prototyping, and usability testing across both web and mobile platforms. Collaborated closely with team members to ensure consistency and refine the overall user experience.

TIMELINE

7 weeks | October – November 2024

THE CHALLENGE

Why create another social platform?

Food lovers like us scatter their recipes across screenshots, bookmarks, and notes apps because existing platforms don't offer a dedicated space for culinary content. Recipe posts get buried in general social feeds, and there's no easy way to organize, revisit, or share them with a like-minded community.

As a student team, we wanted to prove we could identify a real user need and follow a full design process to address it—not just create something that looks pretty. The goal was to deliver a cross-platform experience with a warm, user-centered feel.

HYPOTHESIS

If we create a warm, community-focused space for recipe sharing…

…users will move beyond passive scrolling and actively engage - sharing their own recipes, commenting on others' posts, and building connections around food. We believed that a dedicated platform with the right social features could turn isolated home cooks into a thriving culinary community.

Goals

  • 01
    Structured recipe creation with clear steps and ingredients
  • 02
    Discover new recipes based on personal taste and dietary preferences
  • 03
    Community-driven interactions through sharing, commenting, and messaging

DESIGN PROCESS

From 183 survey answers to a living kitchen community

STEP 01 — RESEARCH

183 foodies spoke. I listened.

Google Form survey → affinity mapping → 3 user personas. Biggest insight: people don’t want another recipe database. They want a friend who cooks.

Affinity map + survey highlights

STEP 02 — WIREFRAMES

Started messy. Got focused.

40+ low-fi screens in Figma. Tested 5 flows with 12 friends. The “Fridge Scan → Recipe” flow reduced decision time by 65% in testing.

STEP 03 — DESIGN SYSTEM

A warm, appetite-inducing system

Logo + 8 custom icons + typography (Playfair Display for headings, Plus Jakarta for body). Warm coral accent (#FE7743) to trigger hunger. Glass-morphism cards for that “fresh ingredient” feel.

15 screens. Zero boredom.

Home Feed

Fridge-based recommendations + trending dishes

Recipe Creator

Step-by-step editor with live collaboration

Live Cooking Room

Video + chat while cooking together
